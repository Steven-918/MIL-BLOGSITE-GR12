Activity 6

Exploring Key Concepts in the Following Topics

1. Legal Terms in Media and Information: These are terms related to the laws and regulations that govern the use and distribution of media and information. This can include terms like "copyright," "fair use," and "plagiarism," which have specific legal implications.

2. Ethical Terms in Media and Information: These terms pertain to the moral and ethical considerations in the use of media and information. Examples may include "transparency," "accountability," and "privacy."

3. Societal Issues in Media and Information: These terms are related to the broader impact of media and information on society. Examples include "misinformation," "media literacy," and "digital divide."

4. Copyright: Copyright is a legal term that grants the creator of an original work exclusive rights to its use and distribution. It covers various media, including text, images, music, and more.

5. Fair Use: Fair use is a legal doctrine that allows the limited use of copyrighted material without permission from the copyright owner. It's subject to certain conditions, such as criticism, comment, news reporting, and education.

6. Plagiarism: Plagiarism is an ethical and legal issue involving the unauthorized use or representation of someone else's work as your own. It's considered a breach of academic and professional integrity.

7. Netiquette: Netiquette refers to the etiquette and manners governing online communication and behavior. It includes terms like "flaming" (hostile online comments) and "trolling" (intentionally disruptive online behavior).

8. Digital Divide: The digital divide is a societal issue concerning the unequal access to information and communication technologies, particularly the internet. Terms within this context may include "broadband access," "inclusion," and "digital literacy."

9. Online Addiction: This refers to the excessive and compulsive use of the internet or online activities, often to the detriment of one's well-being. Related terms might include "internet gaming disorder" and "screen time management."

10. Online Bullying: Also known as cyberbullying, this term refers to the use of digital communication to harass, threaten, or harm others. Terms in this category include "cyberbully," "online harassment," and "trolling."
